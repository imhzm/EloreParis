# Human Content System

Use this playbook whenever SkyWave is producing public-facing content.

## Quality Rule

Public-facing copy reaches full brand quality when it is grounded in real style samples from the user, the brand, or trusted existing assets.

This includes:

- homepage and landing page copy
- service and product copy
- blog posts and articles
- titles and meta descriptions
- FAQ answers
- author bios
- email sequences
- social distribution copy

## Content States

### `sample-based`

Use this state when 2-3 real samples or links are available. Acceptable sample sources include:

- existing website pages
- founder or brand writing
- newsletters
- blog posts
- social posts
- sales pages

In this state:

- derive voice patterns from the samples
- mirror specificity, rhythm, and vocabulary
- use `brand-review` before calling the content final

### `provisional`

Use this state when samples are missing.

In this state:

- create structured and useful copy in a neutral voice
- mark brand-specific polish as pending
- ask once for 2-3 writing samples or links before final polish
- content may still be shipped when the user explicitly approves a neutral voice and factual claims

## Language Rule

Infer the working language from:

- project brief
- target market
- domain and brand context
- existing site copy

If the intended public language is still unclear, ask once before finalizing content.

## AI-Generic Language to Avoid

Avoid generic filler such as:

- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "unlock the power of"
- "seamlessly"
- "revolutionize"
- "elevate your"
- "delve into"
- "transform your business"
- vague superlatives with no proof

Replace filler with:

- specific claims
- concrete outcomes
- clear audience language
- credible differentiation

## Editorial QA Checklist

Before calling public-facing content ready, verify:

- the voice matches the samples or declared brand direction
- the page says something specific, not generic
- the audience and intent are clear
- CTA language is direct and contextual
- entity names are consistent
- FAQ answers are real and not padded
- metadata and headings do not sound templated
- claims are supportable by the business
