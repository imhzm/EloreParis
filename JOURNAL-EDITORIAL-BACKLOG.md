## Purpose

This file turns the Journal from a route that exists into an editorial program that can actually support:

- SEO
- internal linking
- customer education
- product discovery
- conversion support

This backlog is intentionally tied to the current public route graph, not to an abstract blog plan.

What already exists:

## Editorial Pillars

### Pillar 01: Concern-led education

Purpose:

- capture awareness search
- explain symptoms in buyer language
- route readers toward concern and product surfaces

Examples:

- how to choose products for pigmentation without overloading the routine
- how to build a calming routine for sensitive skin
- how to make base makeup last in heat and long days

### Pillar 02: Ingredient explainers

Purpose:

- connect ingredient curiosity to product understanding
- reduce hesitation before PDP visits

Examples:

- what niacinamide actually changes in a daily routine
- vitamin C in morning routines: who it suits and when it feels wrong
- hyaluronic acid myths in hot and dry climates

### Pillar 03: Routine builders

Purpose:

- capture implementation-stage intent
- create direct bridges into routines and PDPs

Examples:

- a simple morning skincare routine for busy schedules
- an evening reset routine for dehydration and dullness
- how to layer makeup prep without a heavy finish

### Pillar 04: Product-choice and shopping guidance

Purpose:

- move users from consideration to decision
- support category and PDP conversion

Examples:

- serum versus moisturizer: what solves what
- how to choose a foundation finish for daily wear
- what makes a beauty set worth buying versus building a basket manually

### Pillar 05: Saudi-context beauty content

Purpose:

- make the Journal locally relevant
- support GEO and trust signals

Examples:

- beauty routines for hot days, dry air, and indoor cooling
- what to pack in a practical travel beauty routine
- how to choose giftable beauty products without guesswork
